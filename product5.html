Ilex crenata, also referred to as Japanese Holly, is a type of holly bonsai that is indigenous to China, Korea, and Japan. Due to its slow growth, compact size, and propensity to form a gnarled trunk, the dwarf pagoda bonsai is a bonsai plant that is frequently cultivated. It has small leaves, thick branching, and a compact structure.

Dwarf Pagoda Japanese Holly bonsai trees prefer acidic, well-draining soil and partial shade. To keep them in shape, they need to be pruned, regularly watered, and treated with an acid-loving fertilizer. The tree can be pruned at any time because it is evergreen, although it is better to wait until it has fully grown.
