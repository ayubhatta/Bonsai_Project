The Money Tree (Pachira aquatica) is commonly used as a bonsai tree due to its thick trunk, large leaves and unique braided stem. It can be wired to shape the trunk and branches, but wiring should be done carefully, as the tree's soft wood can be easily damaged.

They require well-draining soil, and regular watering and fertilization to keep the soil moist, but not soggy. This bonsai tree likes humidity, so misting it often is beneficial to keep the leaves from turning brown.
