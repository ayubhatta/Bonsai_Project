A popular bonsai tree is the Japanese black pine (Pinus thunbergii), a kind of pine tree that is native to Japan. It is an evergreen tree with slender, frequently gnarled trunk, needle-like dark green foliage, and dark green leaves. The tree makes tiny cones, which furthers the illusion that it is a smaller tree.

The Japanese black pine is an excellent choice for bonsai culture because it is extremely resilient, can thrive in a variety of soils, and can tolerate a wide range of growing conditions, from full sun to partial shade. It can be molded into many different designs, such as formal erect, slanting, and cascading, and can withstand pruning and wiring well.
