Acer palmatum, also referred to as Japanese maple, is a tiny tree or shrub that is indigenous to China, Korea, and Japan. Due to its delicate, finely divided leaves, stunning color, and capacity to grow a short, twisted trunk, it is made into a bonsai plant.

As long as the soil is well-draining and there is some shade or sunshine, Japanese green maple bonsai can be grown in a range of soils. The soil should be evenly moist. Overwatering can harm plants by causing root to rot. The best time to prune the tree is in the spring because it loses its leaves in the fall.
